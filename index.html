About Kisumu

Kisumu, also called Kisumu Dala (meaning "Kisumu, home"), is the third-largest city in Kenya and the largest city in the western region. It is a major port city on the shores of Lake Victoria, serving as a commercial and transportation hub for Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, and beyond. Kisumu is the capital of Kisumu County and was once the headquarters of the former Nyanza Province. It is known for its rich Luo culture, scenic landscapes, and vibrant economy.

History

1890s

Kisumu was originally known as "Sumo".

1898

Arrival of British Colonial. The British colonial government identified Kisumu as a strategic location for trade and transportation.

1901

Kisumu becomes a Railway Terminus. The Uganda Railway (now Kenya Railways) reached Kisumu, making it the first inland port on Lake Victoria.

1905

The name changes from 'Sumo' to 'Kisumu'. The British administration restored the name "Kisumu," reflecting its indigenous roots.

1963

Kenya gains Independence, Kisumu played a significant role in Kenya’s independence struggle, with leaders like Jaramogi Oginga Odinga advocating for self-rule.

1969

Kisumu Massaccer, Political tensions escalated between President Jomo Kenyatta and Jaramogi Oginga Odinga, leading to a tragic event at Kisumu Hospital.

2001

Kisumu became a city, The government officially granted Kisumu city status due to its growth in infrastructure and economy.

Why Visit Kisumu?

Lake Victoria & Dunga Beach – Experience the beauty of Africa's largest freshwater lake and enjoy fresh fish at Dunga Beach.
Impala Sanctuary – A serene park with impalas, zebras, giraffes, and a scenic lakeside view.
Kit Mikayi – A mystical rock formation with deep cultural and spiritual significance.
Lwang’ni Beach (Fish Experience) – Enjoy the famous Kisumu tilapia, straight from the lake.
Hippo Point – A perfect spot to watch the sunset while spotting hippos.
Kiboko Bay Resort – A luxurious getaway with breathtaking views of the lake.
Kisumu Museum – Learn about the Luo culture, history, and local wildlife.
Ndere Island National Park – A hidden gem for nature lovers and birdwatchers.
